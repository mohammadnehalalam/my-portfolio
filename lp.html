Light Pollution Research

Assessing Artificial Light Intensity in Dhaka City

Project Overview

Light pollution — the excessive and misdirected use of artificial light at night — has emerged as one of the most overlooked environmental threats of the modern era. In rapidly urbanizing cities like Dhaka, the relentless spread of artificial lighting across commercial zones, hospitals, markets, and public spaces is fundamentally altering the natural night environment.

This research project, conducted by the Falcon X Research Team, set out to systematically assess the intensity of artificial light across key locations in Dhaka City. Using calibrated lux meters, the team collected real-world light intensity data from shops, hospitals, roads, and other high-footfall areas — then rigorously compared those measurements against internationally recognized standards to evaluate the true extent of light pollution in the city.

Beyond data collection, the project goes further — identifying patterns, understanding root causes, and designing practical, evidence-based solutions to reduce light pollution while maintaining urban functionality. The goal is not to eliminate artificial lighting, but to make it smarter, more targeted, and less harmful to ecosystems and human health.

Artificial light intensity measurement across urban zones in Dhaka — Falcon X Research Team

Research Methodology

The Falcon X Research Team conducted field measurements across multiple categories of locations in Dhaka City — including retail shops, hospitals, transport hubs, and residential streets. Lux meters were used to record artificial light intensity at standardized times and distances to ensure consistency across all data points.

Collected data was then benchmarked against internationally accepted standards — including guidelines from the Illuminating Engineering Society (IES) and DarkSky International — to identify locations where light levels significantly exceeded safe or necessary thresholds. Statistical analysis was used to detect patterns and rank zones by pollution severity.

The team also conducted surveys to understand the awareness levels of business owners and residents regarding light pollution, which informed the design of targeted public awareness and policy recommendations.

Key Points

📊

Data-Driven Assessment

Real lux meter readings collected from shops, hospitals, and major public locations across Dhaka City.

🔬

Standard Benchmarking

All collected data was compared against international lighting standards to identify overlit zones and violation levels.

🌿

Ecosystem Impact Analysis

Investigated how excessive artificial light disrupts nocturnal wildlife, plant cycles, and urban biodiversity.

🧠

Human Health Effects

Examined the link between light pollution and disrupted sleep, melatonin suppression, and long-term health risks in urban populations.

💡

Evidence-Based Solutions

Proposed practical, data-backed recommendations including shielded lighting, smart dimming systems, and awareness campaigns.

🏙️

Urban Policy Recommendations

Developed policy proposals for Dhaka city planners to integrate responsible lighting guidelines into urban development frameworks.
